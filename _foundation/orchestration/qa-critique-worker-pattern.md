# QA / Critique Worker Pattern

## Use when

Use this worker when there is already a draft, recommendation, argument, or analysis that needs structured challenge rather than more primary research.

Typical fit:

- recommendation memos
- strategy narratives
- issue trees or business cases
- long-form analytical documents

## Worker contract

The worker receives:

- the full plan
- the draft or approach to critique
- the critique lens or quality bar
- an explicit finding format

The worker does **not**:

- rewrite the whole deliverable unless explicitly asked
- expand scope into new research branches without escalation
- claim ownership of final sign-off

## Expected output

The output should contain:

- prioritized findings
- the logic gap, assumption gap, or missing dimension for each finding
- suggested fix direction
- residual risk if the issue is left unresolved
- process transparency:
  - critique lens used
  - which sections or claims were tested most closely
  - what could not be assessed well and why
  - shortcuts, time-saving choices, or bias risks in the critique

## Quality checks

Before returning, the worker should verify:

- the critique is specific, not generic
- the findings are tied to the plan's objective and quality drivers
- the worker is challenging the logic, not merely restyling the prose
- the process-transparency section explains how the critique was performed, not just what issues were found
